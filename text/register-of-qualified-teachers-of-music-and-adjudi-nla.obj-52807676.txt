PREFACE.
The ASSOCIATION OF MUSIC TEACHERS’ OF
VICTORIA LIMITED performs the important service to the public
of establishing a high ideal among Music Teachers and advancing
the status and dignity of their profession. In the course of so doing,
the Association protects the public by placing in its hands this Register,
which enables parents, school proprietors and others to judge whether
or not teachers are considered by this competent body of experts
PROPERLY QUALIFIED to teach music. No name appears in
this Register without good and sufficient reason for its insertion.
A perusal of the title page will show that the Council is a
representative body whose status and experience indicate their authority
and qualification in the world of music, and their right to compile
the Register contained in the following pages.
Included at the beginning of this Register is a list of examining
Institutions whose certificates or diplomas are obtainable by examina-
tion in Victoria, and are recognised by this Association as providing
a standard which the public can safely accept. A word of warning
will not be out of place here; there are in England, and there may
be in Australia, examining bodies which do not conform to the high
standards of this Association or of experts in the other States of
Australia or in England. Those bodies whose methods are dis-
approved and whose standards are not equal to those this Association
advocates are not listed on this Register. It is not asserted here, nor
are readers to assume, that examining bodies not on oui Register are
unqualified. There may be well-conducted institutions that are not
on this Register, but we emphasise the advice that the presence of
the name of a teaching or examining institution in the following pages
is indubitable evidence of qualification and of satisfactory methods.


THE REGISTER.
The ASSOCIATION OF MUSIC TEACHERS' OF
VICTORIA LIMITED recognises and approves the Certificates
and Diplomas obtainable in Victoria of the following institutions:—
The University Conservatorium of Music.
The Albert Street Conservatorium of Music.
The Australian Music Examination Board.
The Musical Society of Victoria.
The New Conservatorium.
The Associated Board of the Royal Academy and Royal College
of Music; and
Trinity College of Music, London.
The two last-named are the only English musical institutions
examining in Australia that are recognised by the Teachers’ Registra-
tion Council, which, as already mentioned, is the statutory body charged
with the duty in England of admitting qualified teachers in all
branches.
