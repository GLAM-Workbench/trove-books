Premier’s Office,
Sydney, New South Wales,
2nd April, 1900.
Major J. Randall Carey having been the originator of the movement for collecting
funds from the citizens for the purpose of raising and equipping a Bushmen’s Contingent
for service in South Africa, and having first approached me on the subject, asking if
the Government of which I am Premier and Colonial Treasurer would be prepared to
entertain and assist such a movement, and whether they would ask the Imperial Gov-
ernment if such a corps would be acceptable to them; and a Citizens’ Committee having
then been formed for the purpose of bringing the movement to a successful issue, and
that committee having requested the Government to get the organisation, equipment, and
details of the work carried out by the military authorities, I afterwards requested
Major J. E. Carey, as Chairman of the Citizens’ Committee, to take an active part with
Major-General French in the organisation and equipment of the Bushmen’s Contingent
recently gone to South Africa, and I authorised him to go closely into all the expenditure
connected therewith. I caused an account to be opened at the Bank of New South
Wales, so that all moneys handed to me by the Citizens’ Committee should be placed
to the credit of the same, and cheques signed by W. J. Lyne and J. R. Carey be drawn
thereon for all vouchers on account of the expenditure for the said contingent, thus
affording an opportunity of eventually satisfying the committee and citizens how their
money has been expended.
WILLIAM JOHN LYNE,
Prime Minister.
