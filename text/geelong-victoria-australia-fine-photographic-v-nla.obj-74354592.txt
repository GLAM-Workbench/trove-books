GEELONG
The City and its Surroundings.
GEELONG the capital of Victoria?s rich
Western District is in point of size and trade
the second city in the State.
It is ideally situated on the shores of Corio
Bay, the deep-water western arm of Port Phillip,
45 miles from Melbourne. It slopes to the bay on
the northern side, and to the Barwon River on the
South. Its streets are wide and laid out at right
angles, and there are many public and private
buildings of handsome and imposing design.
The City is the Hub of the Western District
and offers the Tourist greater attraction than any
other city in Victoria.
It is surrounded by numerous Holiday Resorts
including borne, Queenscliff, Barwon Heads,
Torquay, Ocean Grove, Anglesea, Portarlington,
and Point Lonsdale.
The City is noted for its Industries.
Numbered amongst them are: Woollen Mills,
Fellmongeries, Cement Works, Freezing Works,
Fertilizing Works, Rope Works, Ice and Salt
Works, Distilleries, and Ford Motor Works.
From an educational standpoint Geelong is the
leading non-capital city in Australia, with its well-
known Colleges and Grammar Schools for boys
and girls, its Technical School, Gordon Institute
of Technology together with the High and State
Schools.
Geelong is a modern city with its ample water
supply, sewerage, gas, and electricity installations.
The Geelong and District has a population of
75,000. (Geelong and suburbs, 43,000).
The Parks of the City are really well worth a
visit. Botanical Gardens with its Eastern Beach
Slopes, Johnstone Park with its fine Civic Centre,
Kardinia Park with its Sports Ovals, and Queen?s
Park with its fine old English trees, stand out
most prominently.
Nature has been kind to Geelong, the views
from Panorama Heights (5 miles), Bell Post Hill
(4 miles), Herne Hill (3 miles), and Leopold Hill
(7 miles) are some of the finest in the State.
Some of the Photographs are by courtesy of the Geelong Publicity Council.
