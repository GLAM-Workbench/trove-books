PREFACE.
On December 15th, 1932, the Sacred Congrega-
tion of Propaganda, in reply to a request from the
Hierarchy of Australia, submitted to the Holy See by
His Excellency the Most Rev. B. Cattaneo, Apostolic
Delegate, forwarded the approbation granted by His
Holiness the Pope for the holding of the IV Plenary
Council of Australia. The Sacred Congregation dir-
ected that a Schema of the matter to be treated and
the decisions to be taken should be prepared and sub-
mitted to the said Congregation for previous appro-
bation. The letter of the Congregation was com-
municated to the Archbishops of Australia on January
17th, 1933.
It was then decided that a Commission should be
set up in each Ecclesiastical Province to prepare the
Schema for the Council.
When His Excellency the Most Rev. P. Bernar-
dini, Apostolic Delegate, arrived in Australia, he
placed his services at the disposal of the Hierarchy
for the preparation of the Schema. The offer was
accepted and in a letter of March 4th, 1934, he asked
the Hierarchy to indicate the principal points that
they desired to have discussed or included in the
Decrees of the Council. With the aid of these sugges-
tions, the Schema was then prepared and submitted to
the Archbishops and Bishops of Australia for com-
ment. It was further revised in the light of these
comments and forwarded to the Sacred Congregation
for approbation. The Schema was approved by the
Congregation of Propaganda, and some new Decrees
were added. These were communicated to the
Hierarchy by His Excellency the Most Rev. J. Panico,
Apostolic Delegate.
These additions were included in the third im-
pression of the Schema, which will be used in the
Sessions of the IV Plenary Council of Australia.


PART I.
THE SESSIONS AND THE WORK OF THE
COUNCIL.
CHAPTER 1.
Those who will be present at the Council.
1. Besides the Apostolic Legate, the following
have the right to assist at the Plenary Council with a
deliberative vote: Metropolitans and Residential
Bishops, who may send their Coadjutors or Auxiliaries
in their stead, Apostolic Administrators of Dioceses,
Abbots and Prelates Nullius, Prefects Apostolic and
Administrators (Vicars Capitular).
2. The other clergy, whether secular or religious,
who are invited to the Council, have only a consul-
tative vote.
3. Each Bishop may appoint a Theologian to take
part in the General Public Sessions of the Council.
4. Each Bishop may have a priest elected by the
Clergy of the Diocese to represent them at the General
Public Sessions of the Council.
5. The Superior of each of the Clerical Religious
Orders and Congregations having a community in
any of the Dioceses of Australia and New Zealand is
also privileged to be present at the General Public
Sessions of the Council. In accordance with No. 2
(above) they will have only a consultative vote.
CHAPTER 2.
The Officials of the Council and their Duties.
(1) The Officials of the Council.


The Officials of the Council are as follows:
Promoters,
Judices querelarum et
excusationum,
Secretaries,
Notaries and Lectors,
Witnesses of the Acts,
Theologians,
Masters of Ceremonies,
Porters.
(2) The Duties of the Officials.
(a) Promoters: The duty of the Promoters is to
see that all that is to be done in the Council
is carried out effectively and without delay.
In particular, they petition that the Council
be commenced; that the Procurators of the
Bishops be not admitted unless they have a
lawful mandate; that the Acts of the Council
be drawn up; that the Profession of Faith be
made; that the Decrees of the Council be
read, etc. Their duties in the Solemn Public
Sessions will be detailed by the Master of
Ceremonies.
(b) Judices querelarum et excusationum: The
duties of the “Judices querelarum et excusa-
tionum” is: (1) To settle any question that
may arise concerning precedence “brevi
manu” in such a way that the rights of all
may be safeguarded. (2) To receive and
give a decision on the excuses of those who
have not attended the Council.
(c) Secretaries of the Council: The duties of
the Secretaries of the Council are: (1) To
see that each of the Fathers of the Council is
informed in writing or orally of the days and


hours of the Sessions, and of the order to be
followed in all the Sessions, except the
Solemn Public Sessions, which are directed
by the Masters of Ceremonies. (2) To
assist the Notaries in collecting the votes of
the Fathers of the Council in all the General
Sessions, whether Private or Public.
(d) The Notaries and Lectors: The duties of the
Notaries and Lectors are: (1) To write the
Acts of the Council, on being requested to do
so by the Promoter at the end of each Ses-
sion. (2) To read the Acts and Decrees of
the Council and whatever else is to be read
in a clear voice and slowly so that all can
understand. (3) To collect the votes of the
Fathers of the Council.
(e) Witnesses of the Acts: After they have
attentively read the Acts of the Council the
Witnesses testify that the Acts have beer
duly compiled and they put their signatures to
the same in testimony of their authenticity.
(f) Theologians: The President'of the Council
may have Consultors in replying concerning
questions of Canon Law and other matters.
(g) Masters of Ceremonies: The duty of the
Masters of Ceremonies is to direct the Cere-
monies of the Council in the Solemn Public
Sessions, and to regulate questions of prece-
dence according to Canons 106 and 347.
(h) Porters: The duty of the Porters is to see
that only those who should be present take
part in the Council and to see that the doors
are closed.


CHAPTER 3.
The Order of the Work of the Council.
The Work of the Council will be done in various
Sessions, which are as follows:
(1) The Preliminary Session.
(2) The Solemn Public Sessions.
(3) The General Private Sessions.
(4) The Private Committee Sessions.
(5) The General Public Sessions.
(1) The Preliminary Session.
This Session will be held in the Chapter Hall of
St. Mary’s Basilica, on Saturday, September 4th,
1937. It will be divided into two parts.
The first part of the Session will begirt at 3.30
p.m. and all those who have a deliberative vote in the
Plenary Council will take part. The business of the
Session will proceed as follows:
(a) The mandates of those who are present at
the Council as Procurators are examined.
(b) The Officials of the Council and the members
of the Private Committees are nominated by
the President, “audito voto Patrum,” and the
Decrees of their appointment duly signed.
(c) The usual formal Decrees, “De modo vivendi
in Concilio” etc., are ordered.
For the second part of the Session all those who
have a consultative vote in the Council are present at
the invitation of the President and proceed to the
places assigned to them.


The President then recites the Prayer “Adsumus,”
and the Apostolic Letter authorising him to call the
Council together and preside at it, is read.
Then the Master of Ceremonies introduces the
Promoters to the President and the Fathers. The
Promoters, after presenting the documents of their
appointment, move the opening of the Council in the
following terms: —
Excellentissime ac Reverendissime Domine,
Reverendissimi Patres; cum concilii plenarii totius
Australiensis ecclesiae, per decretum diei 29 Junii
anni presentis convocati, dies jam instet, cumque, ut
ex instrumentis quae exhibemus, constat, amplitudo
vestra, nos N. et N. jam promotores constituent; pro
nostri officii munere comparentes, instamus et re-
quirimus hoc plenarium concilium de jure et auctori-
tate etiam a Sede Apostolica accepta, rite denuntiatum
atque promulgatum, hoc ipso die per vos deliberari
et declarari incipere jamque incoeptum esse. Cujus
prosperum exoptamus exitum atque a Deo supplices
piis orationibus deprecamur.
The Secretary then reads aloud the following
question: Excellentissime ac Reverendissime Domine,
Reverendissimi Patres, placetne vobis ad laudem
Omnipotentis Dei et Beatissimae Yirginis, S. Patritii,
omniumque Sanctorum honorem, presentem synodum
declarari incipere jamque incoeptum esse?
The Fathers reply, singly, “placet” or “non
placet.” The result is reported to the President, who
hands the Decree of the Opening of the Council to the
Lector, who then reads it.
The President then directs that the Decrees
ordered in the first part of the Session should be read;
after which the President addresses the Fathers on the


manner in which the work of the Council will he car-
ried out. The Oath “de secreto servando” is then
taken if the Fathers think it is expedient.
At the end of the Session the Promoters ask that
the Notaries draw up the Acts of the Session which
are to be signed by the Witnesses of the Acts. The
Prayer of thanksgiving is then recited and all retire,
following the President.
The Most Reverend Ordinaries will assist at this
Session in mozetta and rochet, the Clergy in soutane,
cotta and biretta.
(2) The Solemn Public Sessions.
The Solemn Public Opening Session will be held
in St. Mary’s Basilica, Sydney, on Sunday, September
sth, at 10 a.m. All those who are invited to the
Council will take part in this Session. In addition,
the Clergy, members of the Religious Congregations
and the faithful may be present. (Ceremonies, cf.
Part 11, ch. 1.)
Besides the Solemn Opening Session there will be
two other Solemn Public Sessions.
On Thursday, September 9th, a Solemn Requiem
Mass will be celebrated for the deceased members of
the Hierarchy of Australia and New Zealand. One
of the Archbishops will celebrate the Mass and one of
the Fathers will preach the Occasional Sermon.
(Ceremonies, cf. Part 11, ch. 2.)
The last Solemn Public Session will be held for
the Closing of the Council, on Sunday, September
12th. The Apostolic Legate will pontificate. (Cere-
monies, cf. Part 11, ch. 3)
Choral dress will be worn at these Sessions.
(3) The General Private Sessions.


The first General Private Session will take place
in the Chapter Hall of St. Mary’s Basilica on Monday,
September 6th, at 10 a.m. At the end of each Session
the time of the next Session will be announced.
At the appointed hour the President of the Coun-
cil, the Bishops, and the Officials proceed to the
Chapter Hall. The President occupies the place pre-
pared for him, with the Officials of the Council on
one side and his Theologians on the other. The
Bishops go to the places assigned to them.
(a) The President standing and with head un-
covered recites the “Adsumus.” The Bishops
remain standing, the others present kneel.
(b) The Lector of the Acts reads the Decrees
that are proposed for discussion.
(c) With due reverence to the Holy See and
with the permission of the President, each of
the Fathers of the Council is free to express
his opinion concerning the proposed Decrees.
In order to expedite the business of the
Council this should be done briefly, in such a
manner as to explain what it is desired to
change in the Decrees, why it should be
changed and what words are to be substituted
for those objected to.
(d) So that time may not be lost, the Decrees of
the Schema will be read in Sections and Titles.
After the Decrees of a Title have been read,
the President will ask the Fathers if they are
in favour of the Decrees read. If they are
in favour they will reply: “affirmative.” If,
however, any of the Fathers is not in favour
of a Decree or Decrees, having received per-
mission from the President, he will state his


reasons and propose the changes that he
thinks necessary. The reason and purpose
of the Decree in the Schema will then be
explained and the Fathers will decide if the
Decree in question is to be changed in accord-
ance with the observations made, or whether
it is to remain as it is in the Schema.
They will give their decision by voting,
the votes being collected by the Notaries and
Secretaries in the usual manner.
( e) The Decrees that have not been approved by
the Fathers are to be referred back to the
Private Committee, (cf. no. 4). The Decrees
that are accepted by a majority of the Fathers
are to be again read in the General Public
Session and solemnly approved, (cf. no. 5).
In both cases the Notaries will draw up
the Acts of all that takes place in the Session.
ff) In dealing with the various parts of the
Schema, the Fathers will decide whether it
may be necessary or useful to invite to any
of the General Private Sessions the Repre-
sentatives of the Diocesan Clergy and the
Religious Superiors taking part in the
Council.
The Fathers will assist at these Sessions in
soutane and cincture.
(4) The Private Committee Meetings.
The Private Committees will be appointed by the
President “audito voto Patrum” in the Preliminary
Session.
At the beginning of the Meeting the President of
the Committee will recite the “Adsumus” etc. The
work of the Committee is to re-draft the Decrees re-


ferred back to it by the Fathers of the Council, in
such a way as to express the mind of the Fathers as
manifested in the General Private Sessions. The
President of the Committee will appoint one of the
Theologians present as Notary of the Meeting. The
President will then ask the opinion of the members of
the Committee in turn as to the wording of the
Decrees in question. The Committee will then decide
on the form of the new Decree.
At the conclusion of the Meeting of the Commit-
tee the President or the Notary will give the re-drafted
Decrees to the President of the Council, who will pre-
sent them to the Fathers for approbation in the next
General Private Session of the Council.
(5) The General Public Sessions.
These Sessions will be held in the Chapter Hall
of St. Mary’s Basilica, the date and hour of each being
made known by a Decree to be fixed to the door of
the Chapter Hall. Further notification will be given
to the Representatives of the Diocesan Clergy and the
Religious Superiors by the Secretaries.
At the appointed hour the President of the Coun-
cil, the Bishops, Officials of the Council, the Theolog-
ians, Representatives of the Diocesan Clergy and the
Religious Superiors will proceed to the Chapter Hall
and occupy the places assigned to them.
The “Adsumus” etc. is recited and the Lector
reads the Decrees that have been agreed on in the
General Private Sessions. Those who have a consul-
tative vote may with the permission of the President
make any observations they think opportune. The
Bishops will then give their solemn approbation to the
Decrees by a final vote.
The Fathers of the Council will assist at these
Sessions in mozetta and rochet, the Clergy in soutane,
cotta and biretta.

The deacon invites, as usual, to mental prayer,
which being concluded, the prelate, or other person
appointed for the purpose, ascends the pulpit and
chants the Acclamations, which are responded to by
the whole assembly, the prelates meanwhile wearing
mitres.
Then the prelates, still wearing their mitres,
advance to receive the kiss of peace from the Presi-
dent, after which the solemn pontificial blessing is
imparted, and the indulgences published.
The deacon then chants:
Recedamus in pace.
All answer —
In nomine Christi. Amen.
The procession will then return to the Chapter
Hall in the same order as before.
