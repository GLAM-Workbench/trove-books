LIVERPOOL. TO. AUSTRALIA.
The S S ‘‘Karoola” after a stay of three weeks at Birkenhead
to undergo dry docking and necessary repairs, moved out into
the river Mersey on November 24th. but owing to strong winds
and tide was unable to go alongside. By Sunday evening the
wind having abated sufficiently, we drew alongside the Liver-
pool landing stage. The Liverpool floating landing stage was
originally built to accomodate the worlds largest ships, and is
a mile & a quarter in length, probably the largest and only
one of its kind in the world. As soon as the gangway was put
down, the embarkation of the wounded & convalescent’s began,
and continued until 11 pm, by this time a good number had
been taken on board. The remainder came aboard the following
morning, at !2-3o pro, the ropes were cast off and we swung
into the Mersey, we then realized we had started On our
journey which will eventually terminate in Australia where
the loved ones are waiting to welcome their Boys. After a
fairly good passage, the French, port of Dakar was reached
on December 3rd. The sights of the town was denied us, owing
to the prevalence of fever, and no one was allowed ashore.
After coaling operations were concluded we resumed our trip
on Dec.6th. Father Neptune being kind to us with regards to
weather, we arrived off Capetown Dec 17th went alongside the
following morning. Leave being granted at 2pm, all those who
could were soon in the town, and the blue Hospital suits were
conspicuous everywhere. The trip on the trains to Camps bay,
proved one of the main attractions. The views obtained on the
way, are well worth the time spent in making the journey. We
saw the last of Africa on the evening of the 19th Dec. and our next
port of call will be Fremantle, when it is hoped the “Karoola”
will keep its reputation and land the boys in their homeland
greatly benifited by their trip.
