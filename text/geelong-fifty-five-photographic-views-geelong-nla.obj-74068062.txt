GEELONG ?The City and Its Surroundings
Geelong is the second City in Victoria, and is beautifully
situated between the shores of Corio Bay and the Barwon River,
and has a population of nearly 30,000.
The City is the Hub of the Western District, and offers to
the Tourist greater attractions than any other provincial city
in Australia.
Nature has been kind to Geelong. The views from
Panorama Heights (5 miles), Bell Post Hill (4 miles), Herne
Hill (3 miles) and Leopold Hill (7 miles), are some of the finest
in the State.
Motor Trips are made daily to Barwon Heads, Torquay,
Ocean Grove, Portarlington, and Anglesea.
The City is noted for its Industries ?The Woollen Mills,
Ford?s Motor Works, Cement Works, Cresco Fertilizers, Rope
Works, Tanneries, Ice and Salt Works, are just a few of the
many active industries in the District.
From an Educational standpoint, Geelong is the leading non-
capital city in Australia with its Colleges and Grammar Schools
for Boys and Girls, its Technical Schools and Gordon College,
together with High and State Schools.
The Port is the second for Shipping in Victoria, thousands of
pounds in value of wool, wheat and other products are shipped
to all parts of the Globe.
Geelong has all the advantages of a modern City. Its
Hotels, Cafes and Business Premises are up-to-date in every
respect, and its Public Utilities in the way of Water, Sewerage,
Tramways and Electric Lighting leave nothing to be desired.
The Parks of the City are well worth a visit. Botanical
Gardens, Johnstone Park with the Civic Centre, Kardinia Park,
and Queen?s Park with its old English Trees should not
be missed.
You may reach Geelong from Melbourne (45 miles) by
train in one hour; Boat, 3 hours ; Motors, li hours. There is
a quick service both to and from Melbourne and surrounding
districts.
