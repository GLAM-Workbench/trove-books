FOREWORD
It is hoped that this Exhibition of bookplates designed
by Australian and Overseas artists will interest not only
those who are already acquainted with this branch of
graphic art, but many whose love of books and their deco-
rative get-up prompt them to make a visit of inspection.
The object of the Exhibition is to show that the art of
the bookplate is a living art with national characteristics;
that it is an intensely personal art, reflecting alike the
skill and individuality of the artist and the personality and
tastes of the owner; and that at its best it is closely allied
to the art of printing.
As old as the printed book itself, the bookplate took its
rise in Germany in the 15th century. Its use is now uni-
versal. Originally an etched or engraved impression of the
owner’s armorial bearings, it is to-day predominantly a
pictorial or symbolic representation, in any one of a dozen
reproductive mediums, of personal idiosyncrasy. The pro-
prietorial device of only the noble and scholarly few in
Durer’s day, it now affirms ownership in thousands of
books belonging to readers of all ages of all classes.
It will be noted that Australian designs are in the
majority. This speaks well for the growth and progress
of the bookplate in this country, where it is yet in its
infancy. The work of Adrian Feint, Gayfield Shaw and
George D. Perrottet, to mention the artists whose output
is most considerable and who, in their respective mediums,
are winning the approval of students and collectors abroad,
promises even greater distinction in the future than it has
already gained; and it will be obvious that others besides
them display a flair for this very personal and exacting art.
The foreign contribution will serve to show something of
the technique of designers in older countries, and of their
spirit and humour as applied to the bookplate. The pro-
moters of the Exhibition return thanks to every entrant,
and trust that their work will be a stimulus and an
encouragement to the ever-widening circle of bookplate
enthusiasts in Australia.
To further interest in the subject, it is hoped that
arrangements will be made to show these plates in other
capital cities during the year.
