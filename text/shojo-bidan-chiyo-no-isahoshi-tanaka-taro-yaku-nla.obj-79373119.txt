The following introduction to this little book has been
written by Mrs. Clara Whitney Kaji, who first kindly
showed me the original of this Story, which I, having
found very interesting* and touching, read through again
and again, and de ermined at last to translate for the
benefit of the young readers of this country
The Translator.
PREFACE.
The Story of Mary Jones was published anony-
mously in England several years ago, its object being
to show how through the earnest and persevering-
efforts of one poor girl to obtain the Word of Life,
that great society which now extends its influence in
many lands, and which has caused the Bible to be
translated into about 210 different languages, was first
founded.
A copy of the book having fallen into the hands
of Mr. Tanaka the translator, he was so much affected
by the story that he determined to give others of his
country men the benefit of reading it in their own
language.
The following translation is the result of his
praise-worthy endeavors, and it is sent forth in its new
dress with the prayer that it may continue its mission
with renewed benefit to all who read it.
C. W. K.
Tokyo, Japan.
October 27th, 1898.
