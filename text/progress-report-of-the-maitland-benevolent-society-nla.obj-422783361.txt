The above Committee, in presenting a short report
exhibiting the progress of the above Society for the Half-year
ending October last, beg to express their thanks to the
public generally for the support that has been accorded, by
which much distress has been alleviated, and relief discrimi-
bestowed in East and West Maitland, Bolwarra, and
surrounding districts.
The following statistics, it is conceived, will for the
present convey sufficient information, as to the benefits
dispensed by the Society, to induce the public to contribute
further assistance:—
Number of Persons Relieved—Homan Catholic 14,
Church of England 13, other denominations 10; total, 37.
(With their families included, 101 persons.)
Number of times relief afforded, 89 ; number of loaves
distributed, 262 ; tea distributed 32 lbs., sugar 165 lbs.,
meat 423 lbs., flour 2 cwt.
Greatest total quantity of food given to one applicant—
114 lbs. meat, 57 loaves, 4| lbs. tea, 19 lbs. sugar. Smallest
ditto—2 lbs. meat, 2 loaves, i lb. tea, 1 lb. sugar.
Secretary.
