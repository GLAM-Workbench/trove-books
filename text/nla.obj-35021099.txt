RE NUMBERING OF LOCOMOTIVES.
jt has been decided to re-number and re-classify the whole of the locomotives
in service, and the work will be commenced on 30 August, 1924, and
continued until completed. It will be necessary for all members of the
staff concerned to thoroughly familiarise themselves with the arrangements
which are outlined hereunder. ? ? - :r- D ;
1. All engine numbers will consist of four figures, and all engines of the
same type will he grouped together in order of the date they were originally
placed in service.
2. The class of each engine will be distinguished by the first two figures
of its number, i.e.., 2954 will belong to the 29 class, 5070 to the 60 class, and
so on. This arrangement will operate from the date the engine is re-numbered
from which time the present system of distinguishing (on the Daily Load-
ing Returns, &c.) the type of engine by its letter class is to be discontinued,
and the number only is to be shown. It will, however, still be necessary
to insert an asterisk (*) in front of the number when the engine is fitted with
a superheater.
3. (a) Where there are more than one hundred engines of the same type
in service (as in the case of the present S, P, T, TF, and K classes) the numbers
have been extended into the next or two next hundreds, but in such cases
all engines of the same type will belong to the same numerical class. For
instance, all engines of the present T type will be known as the 50 class
although their numbers will extend from 5001 to 5280. *
*27038?A


( b) As there are different types of the present B, D, and L classes
in service the arrangements provide for each design being distinguished by
a separate class number. For example, engines of the present B type will
be known as the 24 and 25 class, according to whether they are of the old
855 or 8205 type.
The engines shown as the present NN2 type will be placed in service
at an early date, and will be known as the 36 class under the new system.
(c) In consequence of the variations referred to in clauses ( a) and (b)
the classes will not be in consecutive order, and there will be no 31, 33, 51 or
64 classes. |
4. For the purpose of mechanical identification the whole of the engines
have been placed under three groups, and the class number will be prefaced
by a letter to distinguish same (for locomotive purposes only) as follows :
Group 1 Duplicate stock, Construction Branch
(distinguishing letter X). engines and locomotive cranes.
Group 2 Engines which, as the service expands*
(distinguishing letter Z). will be withdrawn from use (arranged
in order of the number of coupled
driving axles with which they are
fitted).
Group 3 Engines other than those in Groups 1
(distinguishing letters C and 2 arranged in order of their
and D.). coupled driving axles, C representing
three, and D four driving axles.
It will not be necessary to quote the distinguishing letters for Traffic
Branch requirements.
5. The highest numbered engine at present in service is below 1500, and in
order to avoid the possibility of any confusion during the time the numbers
are being changed the work will be commenced with the new 15 class (old
D 255 type), and continued until all engines up to and including the 55 class
are completed, after which the 11, 12, 13, and 14 classes will be dealt with.
By this method the staff will immediately realise when dealing with any
engine of a higher numeral than 1500 that it has been re-numbered under
the new scheme.


6. By. reference to the following ? key ? the particular type (old system)
of any engine which has been re-numbered can be quickly ascertained; for
instance, if engine 2401 were being dealt with, the key would indicate it to
be of the 855 type under the old system. Until the staff become thoroughly
acquainted with the new classes it will be necessary to refer frequently to
this key as the present references to the various classes of engines in the
Engine Load Tables will remain in operation until a new book is issued.
When it is required to ascertain the old class of any re-numbered engine
in order to apply the instructions in the Engine Load Tables, this can readily
be done by reference to the key, i.e., 29 class is the type of engine at present
known as the J483.
7. Attached hereto are particulars of :
(a) The new numbers and classes arranged opposite the old numbers
and types (up to 1452), and,
(b) The old numbers arranged opposite the new numbers and classes.
These leaves are to be neatly posted between pages 10 and 31 of the Engine
Load Tables.
The information shown on pages 29, 30, 31 and 32 of the Engine Load
Tables regarding engines 1324 to 1352 and 1453 to 1559 is hereby cancelled.
The engine detail now shown on pages 10 to 31 will require to be amended
in accordance with the instructions issued in the Weekly Notices from time
to time, and care must be taken to see that the necessary alterations are made
in the various columns for the correct engine, i.e., if 5001 is advertised as
being fitted with superheater, &c., the alterations are to be shown for T524
(old number).
