cold water; then set the whole on a slow fire or gas stove. Stir the Composition until it is completely melted, then it will be ready to pour into the
mould. The mould should be heated just hot enough to enable one comfortably to bear one's hand upon it; it should then be carefully greased all
over the inside of the bore (Sperm Oil is best). Place the roller stock inside, but if an iron stock it should be heated first and then wrapped with broad
tape. Do not overheat the moulds —keeping everything warm (but not too hot) means a good face on the roller. Pour the Composition over the
roller spindle; this fills the mould from the centre, and does not wash the grease from the sides of the mould. Pour slowly to avoid air-holes and
flaws. If any Composition is left in the kettle after casting rollers do not let it remain until again casting, but run it into a greased vessel, as
