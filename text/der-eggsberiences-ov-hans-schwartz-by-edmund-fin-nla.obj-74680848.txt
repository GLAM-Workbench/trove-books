A NEW YEAR?S EYE REFLECTION.
With farewell sigh, we speed the parting guest,
Yet while upon his tomb we shed the tear,
New hopes and good resolves inspire each breast,
As with a smile we greet the coming year.
New hopes ! What mortal has not felt their pow?r
As expectation through his bosom thrilled?
And good resolves?forgotten in an hour?
Or if remembered, ?tis as unfulfilled.
New hopes ! How often in the time gone by
Have they brought comfort to the weary brain !
Vain hopes?we now recall them with a sigh ;
We found them baseless ; yet?we hope again !
The good resolves that oh ! so real seemed ?
In all save their fruition quite sincere?
And, that all time-enduring we had deemed,
Unfruitful perished ere the coming year !
New hopes and good resolves, from youth to age,
With each New Year to mankind seem allotted ;
Yet when we glance at Time?s last Annual Page,
We find that, like the previous one, ?tis blotted !
