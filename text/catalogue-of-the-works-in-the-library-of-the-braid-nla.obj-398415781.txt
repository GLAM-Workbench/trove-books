REGULATIONS.
LIBRABY.
1. That the Library shall be open every Monday, Tuesday,
Thursday, and Saturday, from 11 to 1, and from 3 to 5 o’clock.
Also, every Monday, Wednesday, and Saturday evenings, from
7.30 to 9.30.
2. Books can only be obtained through the Librarian, Mem-
bers are held responsible for books so obtained, and if any -work
or works be lost or damaged, the Committee shall determine the
amount of compensation to be paid. The books are not trans-
ferable.
3. Full members are entitled to have from the Library two
works at the same time. Ladies subscribing ten shillings per
annum only one work at a time.
4. Periodicals shall not circulate till the numbers forming one
volume are bound.
5. The periods for which members may keep works are as
follows :
If residing within three miles of Braidwood, 14 days ;
beyond that distance, 21 days.
Any member detaining a work longer than the period
prescribed shall be fined one penny per volume per
day, such fines to be paid before the said member
shall be entitled to any further issue of books.
6. The period allqfed for the perusal of any work may be ex-
tended for seven days longer, provided the -work is produced to
the Librarian for renewal, and has not been inquired for by any
other member.
7, Any member may propose books for addition to the Library
by entering the title, price, and other particulars in a proposing-
book kept for that purpose. The Committee to decide on any
book so proposed.


ROOM.
1. That the Reading-room shall be opened at 8 o’clock a.m.,
and shall remain open until 10 o’clock p.m. every day except
Sunday.
2. That the magazines, periodicals, &c., shall lie on the table
of the Reading-room until the arrival of the succeeding numbers.
That a complete file of the newspapers for the current week be
kept on the table. Members attending the Reading-room are not
to remove any of the newspapers, &c., from the files.
3. That no newspaper, periodical, or hook shall be allowed on
any consideration to be taken from the Reading-room, except by
the Librarian.
4. That any member mutilating, or taking any newspaper,
periodical, or magazine from the Reading-room shall come under
the 10th Rule of the Institute, and be liable to suspension.
5. Conversation strictly prohibited.
6. Smoking will not be allowed under any circumstances,
7. Members to have the privilege of introducing a visitor, who
shall enter his or her name and residence in the Visitor’s Book,
countersigned by the member introducing such visitor or visitors.
8. The Reading-room to be closed on New Year’s Day, the
Anniversary of the Poundation of the Colony, Good Friday, the
Queen’s Birthday, and Christmas Day.

annum ,
