s . “In New Guinea Wilds” is the first moving picture of Australia’s Man-
dated Territory to be presented to Australian audiences, and is in every
way absolutely true to nature. The cameraman responsible for the making
of the film is William J. Jackson, whose portrait appears above. In secur-
i ing his services, The Oriental and Oceanic Pictures Ltd. were fortunate, for
Mr. Jackson has had a long experience of the islands of the Pacific, and
was in New Guinea during the military occupation. His knowledge
of his subject is reflected in the many gems of photographic art to be
seen “In New; Guinea Wilds,” which is the best production of its kind
1 that has beeii screened. It is a classic of motion picture production, and
a credit to those responsible for its being.
