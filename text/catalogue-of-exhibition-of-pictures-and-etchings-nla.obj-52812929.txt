The objects of the Society as set forth in its Royal Charter
(conferred in 1909) are as follows:
The objects of the Society shall be the uniting in one
body Artists of the Empire for the advancement of the
Arts of Painting, Sculpture, Etching, Engraving, Decora-
tion and Architecture, and generally the encouragement
and promotion of those Arts throughout our Dominions,
States and Colonies under the notice of Our Subjects
resident within the United Kingdom by the holding of
periodical exhibitions, AND to do anything incidental or
conducive to the foregoing objects or any of them.
In furtherance of those objects the Council is prepared at
any time to enter into negotiations with Responsible Bodies for
the holding of Exhibitions of works of Art, either Overseas or in
the United Kingdom.
The Council cordially invites Artists of repute, practising
their profession under any of the headings enumerated in the
Charter, both at home and in the Dominions Overseas, to submit
themselves for election as Associates of the Society.
Full information can be obtained on application to

FOREWORD.
The Society, under the title of the Anglo-Australian Soc-
iety of Artists, was fm’i.ded n 1887, by a group of British
painters at the suggest.n and under the Presidentship of the
late W. Ayerst Ingram. R.I In 1889 Her Majesty the late
Qu ;en Vctoria graciously accoided the Society the title and style
of Roya' Li 1904. in response to the sympathy and interest ex-
tended to it from other parts of the Empire, by the authorisation
of His Majesty the late King Edward VII, and in order to
express the enlargement of its sphere of operations, the Society
changed its iitit* to that of the Royal British Colonial Society of
Artists, the late Rt Hen The Lord Strathcona and Mount Royal
shewing his interest in the movement by becoming its first
Honorary President.
His Majesty the late King Edward VII in 1909 was grac-
iously pleased to grant a Charter of Incorporation to the Society,
the Diploma of Members bearing His Majesty’s Royal Sign Man-
ual. and this privilege has been graciously continued by His
present Majesty King George V. In 1921 His Royal Highness
The Prince of Wales graciously consented to fill the position of
Honorary President.
Amongst the earliest supporters of the Society were the
late Lord Leighton. P.R.A., Sir John Millais, P.R.A., Mr. G,
F. Watts, R.A., Sir Ernest Waterlow, R.A., P.R.W.S’., Mr. Henry
Moore, R.A., Mr. J. McNeil Whistler, Mr. E. M. Wimperis, R. 1.,
Mr. T. B. Kennmgton, Mr. Holman Hunt and many others.
The first Exhibition by the Society was held in Sydney in
1899, being subsequently transferred to Dunedin, N.Z. Other
Exhibitions have been held in Melbourne, Montreal, Toronto,
Winnipeg, Jamaica, Cape Town, Durban, Port Elizabeth, Kimber-
ley, Grahamstown, etc 1 .
In the Autumn of 1918 when by reason of the war many
artists from overseas were in Europe, the Society, in association
with the Society of Australian Artists, held a memorable Exhibi-
tion entitled “War and Peace” at the Galleries of the Royal
Academy of Arts (by the courtesy of that body) in which nearly
all the Overseas Dominions, States and Colonies were represented.
In this Exhibition, amongst other Galleries at their disposal, the
large Gallery, No. 3, was allocated to the Australians, and they
filled it nobly.
It is hoped that within no distant future another Exhibi-
tion on similar lines may be possible. Meantime the Council
of the Royal British Colonial Society of Artists is prepared at
any time to enter into negotiations with Responsible Bodies for
the holding of Exhibitions of Works of Art, either Overseas or
in the United Kingdom.
T. C. GOTCH, President.
