Though the last German mail supplies us with
ti� particulars relating directly or indirectly to the
Russian war, except that an English courier
passed through Frankfort with great speed on the
Bth inst., proceeding with dispatches, supposed to
be of a pacific nature, to Constantinople, we have
received some details from other sources which
may be interesting :
Letters from Moldavia speak of the privations
undergone by the Russian troops, in their retr*at.
as having been dreadful in the extreme. The
greater part of the horses had perished, and many
of the soldiers were affected with a contagious
fever, contracted in Bulgaria. It was reported
that General Paskewitsch would have the com-
mand of the grand army of Bulgaria, in the
room of General Wittgenstein ; General Ruclze-
witsch would replace the former in Asia; General
Diebitsch and Prince Scherbatoff have set out for
St. Petersburgh. This last, who directed the
siege of Silistria, is said to have sent in his resig-
nation before the catastrophe.
A letter from Vienna, of the 4th inst. states,
that General Geismar, blocked up in the camp at
Kalafat, had been forced to surrender, after
various attempts to escape. His troops were re-
duced, it is said, to 4,000 men.
If this last statement may be depended upon,
the defeat of the Russians is complete indeed.
General Paskewitsch has been successful in Asia,
and it would seem that to him, as to a last hope,
the tottering fortunes of the Russian army are
committed.
Letters from Berlin of the 6th state, accounts
have been received there from St. Petersburg,
announcing the determination of the Emperor
Nicholas to return to ins army, and to try once
more what his presence there may effect, in the
mean time the Turks are not idle.
Accounts from Constantinople to the 16th ult.
state that the possessions of the late Grand Vi-
zier had been confiscated, and the returns of his
ministry were undergoing strict examination.
The Intendant of Finances, the Kiekaya, and
the Reis Effendi, had also been exiled, charged
with aiding in the malversation of which the
Grand Vizier is accused. Tarlagie Zade, and
three other Ulemas of the first rank, had also
been exiled.
The Reis Effeadi had frequent conferences with
the Sultan in the camp of Rained, and the Camai-
can Pacha, and the Seraskier also frequently held
council with him. The Sultan assisted at the
divans held in the Porte, returning in disguise in
the night.
The continued rains which had fallen for three
weeks had caused the troops in the camp to go
into cantonments, where they continued to exer-
cise, and where the Sultan also resided. It ap-
pears to be his intention to exercise during the
winter the troops arriving from Asia. The
Turkish squadron, which had been stationed
before the Castle of the Black Sea, at the entrance
of the Bosphorus, had re-entered the Strait;
TchapaD Oglou was in the capital, with 22,000
men, and would remain until the Vizier had
completed Id* plans for the retaking of Varna.
It was announced that the Turks had determined
on a winter campaign, but it was thought, if the
rains continued so, this would not be possible.
If wliat we learn from Bucharest can be de-
pended upon, it would seem, however, as if the
Russians anticipated that a winter campaign
would be undertaken by the Turks, for accounts
from that city state that the garrison of Varna is
to be reinforced to 12,000 men, and that a large
force will be placed between Varna and Ba-
jardzik; but we are not told where this force is
to come from. In the present state of af-
fairs, it is not very probable, and situated
as Varna is, we should think that any in-
crease of a garrison would be no more than
an Increase of prisoners to the Turks. The
Nuremberg Gazette informs us that the invest-
ment of Varna has actually commenced, and that
the besieging army amounts to 30,000 men. The
same authority states, that a Turkish force of
20,000 men is at present collected in Silistria.

ORDER TO SUPPRESS PIRACY IN THE
WEST INDIES.
St. Thomas, Oct. 18.?A newspaper of the 28th of
November, contains an order from his Majesty, under
date of the 20th of April, stating that having already
found our endeavours, with those of other maritime
powers, to suppress the piracies frequently committed
on the West Indian Seas, unavailing, a mode of proceed,
ing by which they may be brought to a conclusion as
speedily as possible, is directed, as follows :?.
Ist. Every person found to be engaged in a plot to
fit out piratical vessels or to have done duty on board
of such vessel, as likewise every person having pointed
out booty to a pirate, shall be hanged.
2d. Those who in a less efficient manner have been
accessary in the crime, shah be sentenced to hard la-
bour at the fortifications, for seven years.
