In forwarding to Philadelphia an Exhibit of the Seeds of
Trees, Shrubs, and Plants indigenous to Australia, Tasmania, and
New Zealandthe Exhibitor hopes that it may prove attractive and
afford some information to those who may feel an interest in the
Botanical resources of these distant Colonies. No country is richer
in forests of valuable timber trees, and the beauty and variety of
flowering shrubs and plants can scarcely be excelled in any part of
the world.
The merits of the health-disseminating Eucalyptus Globulus,
or Bhie Gum Tree, are known to every civilized country 07i the
globe. The collection of the seeds of our native trees has become a
most valuable industry; from my places of business at Hobart Town,
Melbourne, and Sydney, I have had collected and sent out, during
the past sixteen months, over two toils of Eucalyptus seeds alone.
This Exhibit, though it includes seeds of nearly all the best
known trees, ore., does not contain one-half the number I have in
stock, and many new species are being annually added to my list.
Mr. Camara, a gentleman of large experience, is my chief
collector.
