MR. WILLIAM BRUCE, PRESIDENT.


Early History.
Bendigo United Cricket Club was estab-
lished on 2bth January, 1861. A club known
as the District Club existed previously, but it
was in difficulties when a meeting was held
on 20th January, 1861, at which preliminary arrangements
were made for the establishment of the Bendigo United.
As a result of that meeting, a number of cricketers
assembled at the Criterion Hotel on 26th January, when Mr.
Pitman was voted to the chair. Mr. J. B. Thompson,
a local pressman, who had been a player of prominence in
Melbourne, read a report prepared by a committee appointed
to inspect several proposed sites for a ground. This com-
mittee, consisting cf Messrs. H. B. Nicholas, J. Symonds,
J. B. Thompson, and Jas. Port recommended the present site
at Back Creek. They advised that ,£l5O be spent in erecting
a two-railed fence all round the ground, the filling up of
holes and a small watercourse, the extraction of stumps,
then the levelling, forming, sowing with grass seed, and
turfing thirty yards square in the centre, and leading the
surface water by another channel on the south side of the
ground. This report was adopted unanimous!}'.
Mr, J. Symonds moved and Mr. Hunt seconded, “That
a committee consisting of Messrs. H. B. Nicholas, J. Fly,
H. Symonds, Bastard, A. Mackay, Allen, Stark, G. Ratcliffe,
Hall, J. Port, J. Symonds, and A. Shum, with power to add
to their number, be appointed to collect subscriptions in
furtherance of the establishment of a Bendigo United Cricket
club and ground ; and that Mr. Bayne be appointed hon.
treasurer and Mr. J. B. Thompson hon. secretary pro tern.”
Carried unanimously.
Mr. H. B. Nicholas moved and Mr. Port seconded that an
application be at once made to the Government through the
district surveyor for a grant of the land at Back Creek.
Carried unanimously.
A formal resolution forming the Bendigo United Cricket
Club was moved by Mr. Thompson, seconded by Mr. Bayne,
and carried unanimously.


A general meeting was held on nth April, xß6i, when
tenders for fencing were accepted, and the committee in-
structed to prepare a code of rules. The following office-
bearers were elected : —President, Mr. L. Macpherson; Vice-
Presidents, Messrs. A. Mackay and H. B. Nicholas; secre-
tary, Mr. J. B. Thompson; and Treasurer, Mr. A. Bayne.
An amateur performance in aid of the Club was given by
members and others at the Lyceum Theatre on 4th July,
1861. An address was written for the occasion by Mr.
Thompson and read by Mr. Hasker.
At the first annual meeting on 24th October, 1861, it was
reported that shortly after the Government grant of six acres
was made a substantial six-feet paling fence was put up
round the ground at a cost of Nine acres were
enclosed. The club had only played one match in the 1860-1
season, which was won. Mr. W. Bruce scored 22 not out
and 1. Mr. A. Shum, with 8 wickets for 16 runs, was the
best bowler.
On 2nd September, 1862, a deputation ■ waited on the
Minister for Lands, Mr. C. G. Duffy, and asked for the per-
manent reservation of the ground. A favorable reply was
received.
At the annual meeting on 4th September, 1862, a special
vote of thanks was accorded to Mr. Angus Mackay for the
trouble he had taken to extricate the affairs ol the club from
difficulty. This was one of many acknowledgments of Mr.
Mackay’s continuous efforts on behalf of the club, in which,
during these earlier years he received able assistance from
Messrs, Macpherson, Nicholas, Bayne, Stewart, Latham,
Barker, Port, Ladams and Hechle, and others.
On 13th October, 1863, the following trustees were
gazetted;—Messrs. A. Mackay, J. Latham, J. Holdsworth
and L. Macpherson. Mr. Latham died recently, and the
present trustees are Messrs. G. Mackay, W. Bruce and
C. Cohen.
In the report read at the annual meeting on 21st September,
1864, reference was made to the erection of “ a very hand-
some pavilion.” Mr. J. Fly was the contractor, and the cost
was 9s.
Amongst other works carried out was the dam at the east
side of the ground, which was for many years the only source
of water supply. In 1872 it is recorded in the minute book
that a vote of thanks be accorded to Mr. John Webster, for
procuring and erecting the chain fence. The tender for the
new fence around the ground was let on sth March, 1883,


the funds for which had been provided by a match between
the Bendigo and Sandhurst football clubs. The ground was
in constant use for football purposes at this period.
Having thus recorded in brief the early history of the
club, I must proceed to refer to the cricketing records, but
before doing so let me direct attention to the unique place
occupied on the list of office-bearers by Mr. William Bruce,
our respected President. For 38 years he has occupied that
position, and the unanimous wish of the members is that he
may fill it for many years to come. A lover of the club and
the game of cricket at all times, and a willing helper in every
time of need, Mr. Bruce wins the regard and esteem and con-
fidence of the members to-day as surely as he did iu days
long past. Many other sturdy supporters of the club in
former years have gone to their rest, and the pages of the
minute books bear testimony to the loss thus sustained.
Before proceeding to deal with the club’s achievements in
cricket, a tribute should be paid to those gentlemen who
have at various times officiated as umpires. It is impossible
to recall every name that is worthy of mention, but those of
Mr. John Devine, Mr. Arthur Wood, Mr. James Dunn, and
Mr. John Stevenson, who gave much time and attention to
the work, will occur to most cricketers. They have all ren-
dered able and conscientious service, and the late Mr. John
Devine won high praise from the members of several visiting
English teams. It is fitting also to refer to the late Mr. M.
Rundell, who officiated as scorer for many seasons, and was
an enthusiastic supporter of the club.

the club, but the principal scorers and bowlers will be found
mentioned in all matches against English and Australian
Elevens, Victorian and other State teams, Melbourne and
Ballarat teams. To include local matches would make the
record too unwieldy, and we are also compelled, owing to
want of space, to omit matches such as those against Castie-
maine, Harcourt, and other teams from districts in the north,
many of which have been amongst the most enjoyable in the
club’s career.
