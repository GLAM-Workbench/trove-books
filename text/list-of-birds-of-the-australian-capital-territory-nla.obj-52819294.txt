FOREWORD.
The publication of this list of birds of the Australian Capital
Territory has been made possible by the temporary residence in the
Capital City of the distinguished ornithologist, Gregory M. Mathews,
C.8.E., F.R.S.E.
Mr. Mathews’s gift to the nation of his entire library, including his
own masterwork in fourteen folio volumes on Birds of Australia, is a
generous act, and one can only regret that his complete collection of
40,000 birds did not also remain in Australia.
Foresters are very much concerned with anything that damages or
destroys the forest, whether it is white ants or fungus diseases,
boring insects and other vermin or anything that causes the loss of
seed and seedlings. In each of these problems birds play a fundamental
part, and generally as friendly agencies, and to know them and their
work is to understand better the ecology of the forest and our great
dependence on bird life.
C. E. LANE-POOLE,
Inspector-General of Forests.
July, 1943.
