AUSTRALIAN COMMONWEALTH
JUBILEE, 1951
On January I, 1901, the six British Colonies
in Australia New South Wales, Victoria
Queensland, South Australia, Western Australia
and Tasmania joined together in an “indis-
soluble Federal Commonwealth. After an exten-
sive testing in peace and war, the Australian
Commonwealth has reached its Jubilee en-
hanced In nationhood, in strength and in free-
dom. The Australia Commonwealth Jubilee is
therefore being celebrated In ail fields of
national activity.
Celebration in the cultural field is not the
least appropriate, for the first significant flower-
ing of distinctively Australian literature and art
during the final quarter of the Nineteenth Cen-
tury was a real force and Influence making for
acceptance by the people of the new federal
union. Federal union in turn has given a new
impetus and quality to Australian cultural de-
velopment. The establishment and development
of photography as one of the arts In Australia,
as throughout the world, has occurred during the
half century of Australian Federation. For that
reason it Is peculiarly appropriate that the Can-
berra Photogaphic Society—for the heart of
the Federal Capital Territory should hail the
Jubilee of the Australian Commonwealth with
an International Salon.
