RULES FOR MENTAL ARITHMETIC.
To find the value of a dozen articles.
Rule.— Consider the pence in the price as shillings, and for
each farthing add 3d.
To find the value of any number oj dozens.
Rule.—Multiply the value of one dozen by the number of
dozens.
When the number of articles does not consist of exact dozens.
Rule.—Find the value of the dozens by the last rule; and
increase or diminish as the case may require, by
the value of the other articles.
To find the value of 20 articles.
Rule, —Consider the shillings in the price as pounds, and
the pence as so many twelfths of a pound.

weight.
Rule.—Multiply 9s. 4d. by the pence in the given price, or
multiply 2s. 4d. by the farthings in the given price.
To find the value of 120 articles.
Rule. —Consider the pence in the price as pounds, and
divide by two.
To find the value of 100 articles.
Rule.—Consider each shilling as £5, and each penny as
Bs. 4d., or, if the price be small, consider every
farthing as so many pence, and their double as
shillings.
The following rule may sometimes be used to ad\an
tage : —Find the value of the given number oi
articles at one farthing, one penny, or one shilling,
and multiply by the number of farthings, pence, or
shillings in the given price.
To find the value of a pound when the price of a hundred
weight is given.
Rule. —Multiply the shillings in the price by 3, and divide
by 7, and the result will be the value of one pound
in farthings.
To find the interest of any number of pounds for any number
of months at 5 per cent, per annum.
Rule. —Consider the pounds as pence, and multiply by the
number of months.
To find the interest for any number of days at 5 per cent.
per annum.
Rule.—Multiply the principal by the days, and divide the
product by 3; or, multiply one of them by a third
of the other; cut off the unit figure, and deduct a
penny for every 65.; the result will bo the answer
in pence.
To find the interest for any number of months at 6 per tent.
per annum.
Rule.—Multiply the principal by the months, cut off the
unit figure, and the remainder will be the answer
in shillings. The unit figure increased by a filth
of itself will give the pence.
