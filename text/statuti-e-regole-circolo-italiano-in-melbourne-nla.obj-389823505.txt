Oggetto e Organizzazione del Circolo.
i, —Si è costituito in Melbourne un Circolo Italiano che
si propone i seguenti scopi :
A. Provvedere un luogo conveniente di ritrovo per gli
Italiani residenti o di passaggio in Melbourne, e per
quelle persone di qualunque altra nazionalità che
amino di associarsi coi medesimi ;
B. Rappresentare, promovere e difendere gli interessi
italiani in questa Colonia e in tutte le altre dell’
Australia ;
C. Cementare la comunicazione di idee, progetti e
relazioni scambievoli ;
D. Soccorrere e consigliare gli Italiani infermi o senza
impiego.
2. —Esso è rappresentato a tutti gli effetti da un Comitato
composto come è detto all’ art. i 6.
3, —Provvede alla sua vita ed al conseguimento dei suoi
scopi colle contribuzioni obbligatorie e volontarie dei soci
e con tutti gli altri mezzi che il Circolo crederà bene di
adottare.
4. —I suoi membri si distinguono in effettivi e aggregati.
I soli soci effettivi hanno voce consultiva e deliberativa, e
diritto di eleggere ed essere eletti alle cariche del Circolo.
Ammissione al Circolo
3. —Nessuno può essere ammesso come socio effettivo se
non è cittadino italiano.


Object and Organisation of the Club.
,___The above named Club has been formed in Mel-
bourne for the following purposes :
A. To provide a suitable place of re-union for Italian
residents in or visiting Melbourne, and for
such persons of other nationality, who may wish to
associate with the same ;
B, To represent and promote Italian interests in this
and in all the other Colonies of Australia;
C. To initiate the mutual exchange of ideas, projects
and intercourse ;
D. And to furnish help and advice to such Italians
(new arrivals in the colony) as they may require.
2. —The Club is presided over and managed by a Com-
mittee composed as per article 16.
3. —Funds for carrying out the different purposes of the
Club will be raised by ordinary fees and voluntary
contributions.
4. —The members are classed as voting and non-voting
members.

Contribuzioni dei Soci.
12. —Ogni socio deve pagare una tassa d’ ammissione e
una tassa annuale, anticipata d’ anno in anno.
13. —La tassa d’ ammissione a socio effettivo è di £ st.
due, ed a socio aggregato di £ st. una ; e quella annuale

6.—Each candidate for admission must be proposed by
one member and seconded by another, and the name of the
candidate, together with those of the proposer and
seconder, the latter in their own handwriting, must be
exhibited in the reading room of the Club for at least
fifteen days, preceeding the ballot.
7. —At the end of these fifteen days the members will be
—by the Committee—called on to vote for the admission
of the candidate.
8. —If three negative votes are given, the candidate will
not be admitted as member.
9. —Italians visiting the colony will be admitted
to the Club on a request forwarded to the Committee
signed by one or more members, and on such conditions
as the Committee will see fit.
10. — Non-voting members will be admitted under the
same rules as per articles 6,7, and 8.
11. —The Secretary will notify to each candidate his
admission to the Club, and furnish him at the same time
with a copy of the Rules and Regulations of the same.
Fees.
12. —Each member will pay an entrance fee, and a yearly
fee in advance.
13- —The entrance fee for voting members is two
pounds sterling, and for non-voting members one pound
sterling ; the yearly fee for voting members is three
pounds sterling, and for non~voting members two
pounds sterling.


14. — Il nuovo socio, che dopo fattagli la dovuta partecipa-

radiato dal Ruolo dei membri del Circolo, e decadrà on

narie circostanze da apprezzarsi dal Comitato.
Il nome del socio radiato sarà affisso nella sala coll’ anno-
tazione : radiato per mancanza ai propri impegni.
Rappresentanza del Circolo.
16.—1 l Circolo è rappresentato e diretto da un Comitato
composto di un Presidente, un V. Presidente, un Segretario
e Tesoriere e sei Consiglieri, eletti a maggioranza assoluta
di voti ogni anno nell’ assemblea generale.
17. —In mancanza o impedimento del Presidente e V.
Presidente, ne farà le veci il più anziano tra i membri del
Comitato.
xB. Se nel corso dell’ anno qualche vacanza si verifica nel
seno del Comitato, esso è autorizzato a provvedervi fino
alla prima riunione generale, eccetto il caso della carica
del Tesoriere.
19. Al Comitato è affidato in tutto e per tutto il buon
andamento del Circolo; e gli è concesso di poter far rego-
lamenti, dettar norme e discipline interne purché non si
oppongano a quanto è disposto nei presenti Statuti e Regole


i - —If a newly elected member does not pay his fees
within fifteen days of his election, it shall be void, unless
he can justify the delay to the Committee.
15. —If a member does not pay his fee, and after having
been invited to do so, does not do so within thirty days, his
name will be erased from the list of the members of the
Club, unless he can give valid reasons to the Committee
for the delay.
The name of any member who shall thus have been
erased will be posted up in the reading room with a memo,
stating : erased for having failed to meet his liability.
Management and Direction of the Club.
16.—The Club is managed and directed by a Committee
composed as follows : —A President, Vice-President,
Secretary and Treasurer, and six Councillors elected once,
a year by the general assembly of the Club.
17. —In the absence of the President and of the Vice-
President their place will be temporarily filled by the oldest
councillor present.
18.—If during the year a member of the Committee
should retire, he can be replaced by the Committee itself
for the remaining portion of the year, except in the case
of the Treasurer.
ig.—The Committee have the general care of the Club,
and have power to make by-laws and to alter or rescind
the same as occasion may require, such by-laws to have
the same effect as —and shall not be opposed to —the
Rules,

Meetings.
20.—0 n the first of February of every year at eight
o’clock in the evening, a general meeting of the members
of the Club will be held to receive the report of the Com-
mittee—to examine the accounts —to make new rules
or alter the old ones, and to treat and discuss all matters
of which notice shall have been given.
21. —The Committee can at any time convene a general
meeting by a circular signed by at least three members of
the said Committee, and giving notice of the subjects
which will be submitted to the general meeting.
The Committee will also call a general meeting on a
request signed by at least six members of the Club.
22. —At least fifteen members of the Club must be
present at the general meeting to make its proceedings
valid.
In case it should be necessary to call a second general
meeting (for the same objects) in consequence of a want of
sufficient number of members on the first occasion, any
number of members will render the deliberations of the
meeting valid.
23. —All resolutions taken by the general meeting of the
members of the Club will be binding upon all the members,
whether they were present or not at the said meeting, if
this has been convened according to the established Rules.
24. —Whenever there will be a surplus from the revenue
of the Club, a general meeting shall be called to deliberate
on the disposal of this surplus.
25. —No new rules can be made, altered, or suspended
unless notice has been given fifteen days before the meeting

Expulsion of Members.
28.—Not only the members who have not paid their
dues to the Club, but also those who may have been
proved to have wilfully broken its Rules and Regulations,
or who should conduct themselves in or outside the Club
in an unbecoming and ungentlemanlike manner are
liable to expulsion.
When the attention of the Committee is drawn by
any member to the conduct of any other member, notice
must at once be given to the latter, and if the Committee
after proper investigation, find that the facts of the case
deserve the consideration of the members of the Club,
a general meeting shall be called in the usual way, taking
care that the accused member be specially informed of
this. The assembled members shall vote by ballot and
two thirds of those present are necessary to render the
expulsion valid. The result is at once to be notified to the
accused member.
