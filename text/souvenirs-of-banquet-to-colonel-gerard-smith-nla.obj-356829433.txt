BANQUET
Colonel Gerard smith,
Governor-Designate of Western Australia,
AT THR
Imperial Institute,
On Monday, November iith. 1895,
At 7.30 p.m. precisely.
The Right Hon. JOSEPH CHAMBERLAIN, M.P.,
Secretary of State for the Colonies, in the Chair,
Evening Dress, with Insignia of Orders and Miniature Decorations, will be worn.
Guests will arrive at, and depart from, the Main Entrance of the Institute.
On arriving at the Main Entrance, Guests should turn to the left and proceed to
the Council Chamber at the end of the West Corridor, on the right-hand side. This will be
the Cloak Room.
The British-American Conference Room (immediately opposite the Council Chamber)
will be reserved for the use of the Committee.
Dinner Table Plans will be given out in the British - Australasian Conference Room
(next adjoining the Committee Room).
Guests, after obtaining these Plans, should return along the Corridor and enter the
Reception Vestibule by the West Door and Aisle.
It is particularly requested that Guests (other than those specially invited by the
Committee of Invitation) will, after being received, proceed up the Main Staircase to their
seats in the Great Hall.
The Bijou Orchestra will play in the Balcony of the Vestibule during the Reception
and in the Great Hall during Dinner.
The West (Smoking) Foyer will be open during the evening, and devoted after Dinner
to the use of the Guests.
Cabs will be on call throughout the evening,
Arrangements have been made with the Metropolitan District Railway Company that
the subway from the South Kensington Station shall be kept open from 6.30 p.m. to 11.30 p.m.,
and railway tickets which are available at South Kensington will be recognised as also available
for the subway.
By order of the Committee
C. BETHELE,
Hon. Sec.
