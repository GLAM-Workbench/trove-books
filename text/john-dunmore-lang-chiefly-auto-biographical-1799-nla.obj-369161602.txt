APPRECIA TIONS
(Typical of many received since the issue of the first volume in March.)
Mr. Justice John A. Ferguson, Judges’ Chambers, Sydney, author and compiler of the several volumes of the
“Bibliography of Australia”: “Those of us who know of the efforts of Mr. Archibald Gilchrist to ascertain and
record all authentic information regarding Dr. John Dunmore Lang, the great Australian patriot, and his many
activities,- have been gratified by the production of a work invaluable to the student and to any future
biographer, replete as it is with facts of interest to any reader and supported by references and authorities.
Here we find a true picture of the churchman, educator, pioneer, nation builder and statesman, which will
stand for all time.”
Dr. George Mackaness, 0.8. E., author of “Governor Bligh” and other front-rank biographies: “Your very handsome
first volume of J. D. Lang has just come to hand and I have spent a most enjoyable hour or two just skimming
the pages. I must, and most appreciatively, congratulate you on the successful completion of so tremendous a
task as that you have now accomplished. As .one who has spent many years in somewhat similar research, I
am probably as well qualified as anyone to realise the immensity of the task you undertook in bringing together
in an organised way the cream of the Lang material. I look forward with great Interest to volume two.”
Dr. C. H. Currey, Sydney University: “I’ve just finished reading the first volume of your work on Doctor Lang,
and I write to thank you for it. The material you have selected and ordered not only serves to give an
impression of John Dunmore Lang as nothing else about him known to me approaches, but must be invaluable
to the historian who would attempt an appraisement of him—‘warts and all.’ As I am lecturing in Australian
history at the university this year, you can imagine how I appreciate the spade work you have done and how
eagerly I look forward to the second volume. Indeed I cannot have this too quickly. Best wishes for every
encouragement in your valuable and valued undertaking.”
Rev. Lindsay Lockley, M.A.. 8.D., Principal, Queensland Congregational College: “I express very real appreciation
for a book that gripped me, and am looking forward to volume two with keen anticipation. What a grand
job you have done. I had not realised before that it was possible to keep a story running so smoothly when
it was made up of extracts connected by a minimum of editorial comment. That surely is a real accomplish-
ment. I found the story so gripping that I took the book to bed with me. And anyone who will recline in
, bed with 370 foolscap pages on his chest must be showing appreciation of a book’s merits.”
Rev. Frank Hanlin, M.A., 8.D., Editor, “N.S.W. Presbyterian” newspaper; You have done an excellent job. The
admirable planning and documentation speak for themselves. You have done a tremendous amount of valuable
research work and all serious students of Australian history must be grateful to you.
